One of my favourite things about doing Eat the Strip is visiting places that don't fit neatly into a category.

Heaven Inn Devon is a café. It's also an inn. There's a salon on the property. Outside sits an ice cream truck. Depending on when you visit, you might see people checking into a room upstairs, grabbing lunch, getting their hair done, or sitting on the patio enjoying a coffee in the sun.

The property sits on St. Marys Street on Fredericton's Northside, just a short distance from the walking trails. On a beautiful Thursday morning in June, Brooke, Dawoon and I stopped in to see what all the fuss was about.

Entering through the side door, you're greeted by a bright and welcoming café space. There are only a handful of tables, with much of the business seemingly geared toward takeout, but that's part of the charm. Sketches hang on the walls and a large mural depicting a map of the Northside stretches across one side of the room.

We grabbed a table near the back as regulars filtered in and out.

Brooke immediately commented on how cozy the space felt. She also appreciated that the prices felt fair for what was being offered.

Over the next hour, it became clear that Heaven Inn Devon appeals to just about everyone. Construction workers stopped in for coffee. Retirees gathered around tables chatting with one another. Young parents came through with children in tow. Others simply grabbed a drink and headed back out the door.

Many people were wisely taking advantage of the patio.

Serving them all was Paige.

"I love it," she said of working at Heaven Inn Devon. "I don't think I've ever had a boss in my life that I've loved that much."

In fact, she told us that the owners had even offered to help her move.

"We have a very good community. We have a lot of regulars and they're amazing." She paused before adding another thought. "I don't think we'd be what we are without our regulars."

That sense of community seemed evident throughout our visit.

Paige recommended the honeycomb ice cream from the truck outside, explaining that the toffee crunch mixed into it is what makes it a customer favourite. On this visit we stuck to the café. 

She also pointed us toward the banana bread latte, a seasonal specialty that she said she can usually make even when it's not officially on the menu.

For drinks, Brooke ordered the salted caramel mocha. The whipped cream was generous without becoming excessive and the coffee itself still managed to shine through.

Dawoon went with an iced vanilla matcha.

According to Dawoon, it hit the sweet spot for sweetness and tasted exactly how a good matcha should.

I ordered the banana chai latte.

The banana flavour could have easily overpowered everything else, but instead it complemented the chai spices surprisingly well. Cold, refreshing and generously portioned, it was exactly what I wanted on a warm June morning.

As good as the drinks were, we still needed something to eat.

The chicken pesto naan sandwich featured soft naan bread, juicy chicken and plenty of melted cheese. It was filling without being overly heavy and made for a satisfying breakfast.

![Soft naan packed with juicy chicken, melted cheese and pesto makes for a hearty lunch that avoids feeling overly heavy](/uploads/img_1764.jpeg "Heaven Inn Devon naan pesto")

The ham and cheese croissant was equally enjoyable. The croissant itself was flaky and buttery while the cheese delivered the kind of satisfying stretch you'd hope for. Both sandwiches came with chips, which rounded things out nicely.

One thing we didn't manage to get our hands on was a scone.

Apparently that happens a lot.

While we were there, customers periodically stopped in looking for them only to discover they had already sold out. Some menu items develop a reputation because businesses tell people they're popular. Others become popular because customers keep showing up asking where they went.

Later, I spoke with co-owner Jen about the business.

Jen's professional background is in real estate, though before that she spent years working as a server, restaurant manager and at a golf course. She and Darren purchased the property in 2020, beginning a renovation process that ultimately took roughly three years.

Today, the business has settled into a rhythm of serving both longtime regulars and newer customers.

"I think the older people really like it because of the history of the house and Paige has come up with newer drinks for the younger generation."

Recently, the café has also started attracting more high school students.

"I like it when Paige makes all kinds of new things," Jen said.

Like many businesses in the hospitality industry, Heaven Inn Devon faces challenges from rising food and labour costs.

"The restaurant industry is a difficult industry to be in," she said. "Our goal is to stay where we are."

Fortunately, owning the building provides some stability.

Most importantly, people continue to show up.

"We've gotten great community support. We have lots of regulars that come in."

One challenge remains.

"I think sometimes people still think it's not open to the public and just to people in the Airbnb."

That's understandable.

At first glance, Heaven Inn Devon looks like an inn that happens to have a café attached.

In reality, it's become something closer to a community hub.

For my chicken pesto naan sandwich and banana chai latte, the bill came to around $20. That's perhaps a touch higher than some of the lunches we've covered, but the atmosphere, service and quality justify the extra few dollars. While the food is certainly good, the drinks are what stood out most during our visit. A regular latte runs around $5, while my banana chai latte came in around $6 and was more than generous in size. Both Brooke and Dawoon were equally happy with their drinks.

More than anything, though, Heaven Inn Devon feels comfortable.

Maybe that's because of the historic house. Maybe it's the steady stream of regulars. Maybe it's because the staff seem genuinely happy to be there.Whatever the reason, it's easy to see why people keep coming back. As for me, I'll be back for the scones.

Assuming I can get there before everyone else buys them.
